Physical units astrophysics

A physical quantity is something that can be measured. For any measurement, the unit used must be indicated to give an idea of ​​the scale of the measurement.

For example, distance can be measured in kilometers or miles. They are similar, but not the same, and it is important to identify which one was used for the measurement, so that you know what the distance really is.

International System of Units :

Units used by scientists around the world are standardized in the Système Internationale d'Unités. It is important to remember these six fundamental units of measurement.

- Metre (m) – unit of length

- kilograms (kg) – unit of mass

- Second (s) – unit of time

- Ampere (A) – unit of electrical current

- kelvin (K) – unit of temperature

- Mole (mol) – unit of the amount of substance



- Frequency - hertz

- Force - newton

- Energy - joule

- Power - watt

- Pressure - Pascal

- Electric charge - coulomb
